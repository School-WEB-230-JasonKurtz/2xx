The Holy Bible (NIV)
The Bible is a collection of sacred texts or scriptures that Christians consider to be a product of divine inspiration and a record of the relationship between God and humans. The Christian Old Testament overlaps with the Hebrew Tanakh and the Greek Septuagint. The New Testament is a collection of writings by early Christians, believed to be disciples of Christ, written in first-century Koine Greek. These early Christian Greek writings consist of narratives, letters, and apocalyptic writings.
